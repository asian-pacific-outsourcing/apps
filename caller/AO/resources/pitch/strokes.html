Strokes

-The purpose of this first trade is to earn your trust so we can move on to larger and more profitable positions.

-I know you see the profit potential but you’re letting your fear of something new cloud your judgment and get in the way of you making money.

-Everyone knows the key to making money in the markets is price and timing! That’s why I’m calling you today on ____.

-News moves markets anticipation makes a stock climb higher!

-In order for you to make an educated business decision, you need to have all the facts in front of you fresh in your mind.

-Almost all of my clients started out with the same questions and concerns, but what they found after our first trade was that everything was answered with proven results.

-Another email isn’t going to make you any money, I am.

-Our business is like your business, we’re always looking for more clients!

-Most of my clients have started the same way, having little or no experience in the markets. That’s ok! My job is to guide and advise you every step of the way.

-Its human nature to be nervous when starting something new. That’s why I recommend we start small, before moving on to bigger business.

-If I don’t impress you, close your account and don’t do anymore business with me. But when I out perform any other investments you have, I will expect more and larger business in the future.-

-I know you weren’t expecting my call today. If I could have called you months ago and told you to expect my call I would have, but unfortunately that’s not the way the markets work.

Softening Statements

*ALWAYS begin a stroke/rebuttal with a softening statement*

- I can appreciate that…

- I understand your view point…

-I don’t blame you for feeling that way…

-Many people felt just as you do…

-Yes, I agree…

-You no doubt have good reason for feeling that way…

-That is a perfectly natural thought…

-I can see why at first glance you would think that…

 

Not Interested

  • I can appreciate your not interested at this time right now, but if you can give me a few moments of your time, RIGHT NOW, you’ll have all the information to base your decision on and we can part ways professionally. Is that fair enough?

  • I’m not trying to convince you to do something you don’t want to do. All I’m looking for is a few minutes and an open mind. If you like what I have to say, great, maybe we’ll do business! If not, then we’ll simply part as professionals. That’s fair, right!

  • From one business man to another, if someone presents me with a profitable business opportunity I at least here them out. The information I’m going to share with you is free. Take a few moments to decide if it’s for you. Not on a personal level but on a business level. OK?

  • It sounds to me like you may have had a bad experience in the past. Is that correct? (If yes) I’m sorry to hear that, allow me to explain what we have and if it’s not for you that’s fine, we won’t contact you again.  (If no) What is it you have against listening to a profitable business idea?

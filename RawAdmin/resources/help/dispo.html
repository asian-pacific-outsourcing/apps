Contact Dispositions

Contents

 

Every contact has a disposition that reflects his status within the system. Each disposition has a code, a description, and a database representation. Freshly mined, raw contacts have a nil (0) disposition. Disqualified contacts are stored in the database as a negative number. As a client progresses through a qualification process to ultimately become a client, his disposition will generally take on higher positive values. This allows the system to more efficiently process the contacts data.

NOTE: Although the database values are given below, it is STRONGLY RECOMMENDED that you do not directly manipulate these values in the data as you may corrupt the overall state of the contact record. This is because the disposition works in conjunction with other fields such as callback date, page number, and hard to reach count to completely describe each contact's status.
